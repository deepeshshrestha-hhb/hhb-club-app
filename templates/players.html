Current counts points from the last 3 years (2024–2026) only — the default ranking, rewarding recent form. Cumulative totals points from every tournament in club history.

Your HHB Score is the sum of points earned across all three of our annual club tournaments. Each tournament carries a different tier weight — just like the Gold, Silver, and Bronze grading system used by Badminton Scotland and the BWF to rank tournaments by strength.

🥇 Gold — Championships Pool A

Features the club's strongest players. Winning or placing here is the hardest competitive achievement at HHB, so it earns the most points.

🥈 Silver — Doubles Classic

Our flagship annual event and the established scoring baseline. Open to all ability levels in a group-stage plus knockout format.

🥉 Bronze — Championships Pool B

The entry-level and developing-players pool. Results here count towards your HHB Score but are weighted lower, so strong performances against tougher competition are always rewarded more.

The exact ratio between tiers is taken directly from the Badminton Scotland / BWF Gradings and Values 2026 document: Gold : Silver : Bronze = 30 : 14 : 7 (Gold is ×2.14 Silver; Silver is ×2 Bronze). The HHB points table below was derived from those same ratios, rounded to the nearest 5, so the weighting isn't arbitrary — it mirrors how the sport's own governing bodies value tournaments of different strength. The Players League sits outside the Gold/Silver/Bronze tier system as it runs across a full season rather than a single event, but its points methodology is deliberately aligned with Gold. This is because the League is the truest test of badminton ability and skill at HHB — players accumulate points by winning matches with a rotating set of different partners, so results cannot be carried by one strong pairing. Finishing high in the League demonstrates consistent, all-round playing strength.
